Format & Rules

Match format

  • Every match is a best of 3 sets.
  • Rock paper scissors decides who picks their side.
  • Random stage every game.
  • Winners record the result on the Dashboard right after the match (mis-clicks can be undone).

Phase 1 — Bracket & Round Robin

  • Bracket: 8 players, single elimination. The bracket champion becomes the Big Champ.
  • Round robin: 4 players, everyone plays everyone. Most wins becomes the Little Champ.
  • Round robin ties for first are settled by tiebreaker rounds — every tied pair plays, wins count toward the standings, and rounds repeat until one player stands alone on top.
  • Both run at the same time on different stations.

The Gauntlet

  • The Big Champ holds the top throne; only the Little Champ can face them, in a champ challenge.
  • Anyone else can challenge the Little Champ by joining the challenge queue from the Dashboard.
  • Beat the Little Champ and you take the title — a champ challenge is then automatically queued next up (your immediate title shot) and again at the end of the queue.
  • If the Little Champ beats the Big Champ in a champ challenge, they swap titles and a fresh champ challenge is queued at the end of the line. Champ challenges never count against anyone's challenge limit.
  • One shot per champ: you may only ever challenge a specific person once while they hold Little Champ. Challengers who've already had their shot are removed from the queue once a champ challenge settles who holds the title.

How it ends

  • The Gauntlet runs on a 1 hour clock. When time expires, the reigning Big Champ wins the tournament.
  • It also ends early if the Big Champ wins two champ challenges against the same reigning Little Champ — only a title swap resets the count.
